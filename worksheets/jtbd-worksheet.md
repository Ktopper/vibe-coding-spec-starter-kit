# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) Worksheet
## Understanding What Your Users Actually Need

The JTBD framework helps you understand the true "job" your app is being "hired" to do. This isn't about features‚Äîit's about understanding the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of your user's needs.

---

## üìñ THE FRAMEWORK

Every product serves three types of jobs:

1. **Functional Job:** The practical task it completes
2. **Emotional Job:** How it makes the user feel
3. **Social Job:** What using it says about the user

**Example: Spotify**
- Functional: Play music
- Emotional: Feel entertained, discover new music, nostalgia
- Social: "I have good taste in music"

## üé® AESTHETIC IMPLICATIONS

Your JTBD analysis should inform design choices:

### If the emotional job is "calm/peaceful":
- Use soft colors, generous whitespace
- Avoid busy animations, loud notifications
- Prioritize simplicity over feature density

### If the emotional job is "confident/empowered":
- Use bold typography, clear CTAs
- Show progress indicators, success states
- Celebrate user actions

### If the emotional job is "in control":
- Give users choices, customization
- Show what's happening (no mystery)
- Allow undo, provide escape hatches

## üìö COMPLETE EXAMPLES

Below are two filled-out JTBD analyses so you can see what "good" looks like.

### EXAMPLE 1: Focus Recipes (Timer App)

**Part 1: Functional Job**
When I **need to focus on important work**, I want to **follow a structured work session with breaks**, so I can **actually finish my work without burning out**.

**Part 2: Emotional Job**
My app makes users feel **in control and accomplished** because it **breaks down overwhelming work into manageable phases with clear progress**.

Top 3 Emotions: In control, Accomplished, Relieved (anxiety reduction)

**Part 3: Social Job**
Using my app signals that I am **serious about my productivity and intentional about my work habits**.

**Time-to-Value:** 10 seconds (Open ‚Üí See recipes ‚Üí Tap one ‚Üí Timer starts)

**Design Implications:**
- Full-screen timer (minimize distractions)
- Breathing animations (reinforces calm/control)
- No statistics dashboard (adds complexity, not value)
- Bottom-placed controls (thumb zone)

---

### EXAMPLE 2: Resume Roaster (AI Wrapper)

**Part 1: Functional Job**
When I **apply for competitive jobs**, I want to **check if my resume is actually competitive**, so I can **improve it before real recruiters see it**.

**Part 2: Emotional Job**
My app makes users feel **prepared and confident** because it **exposes weaknesses before employers see them**.

Top 3 Emotions: Confident, Prepared, Smart

**Part 3: Social Job**
Using my app signals that I am **thorough, detail-oriented, and serious about my career**.

**Time-to-Value:** 42 seconds (Open ‚Üí Paste resume ‚Üí Click ‚Üí Wait 5s ‚Üí See results)

**Design Implications:**
- Dark theme with red accents (edgy "roast" aesthetic)
- Large text area for full resume (functional priority)
- Numerical score (concrete benchmark, confidence boost)
- NO gentle language (honest = trustworthy = prepared feeling)
- Progress messages during AI processing (reduces perceived wait)
